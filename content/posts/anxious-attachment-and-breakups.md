You already knew the relationship was over. And yet the pain feels completely out of proportion - the obsessive thoughts, the inability to function, the urge to reach out that does not quit. You watch other people move on and wonder what is wrong with you.

Often, the answer is anxious attachment - and understanding it changes how you approach recovery.

## What anxious attachment actually is

Attachment theory, developed by John Bowlby and expanded by Mary Ainsworth, identifies different patterns in how people relate to close others. Anxious attachment develops early - typically when caregiving was inconsistent, leaving the child uncertain about whether their needs would be met. The nervous system learns to stay hypervigilant about the relationship, monitoring for signs of distance or abandonment.

In adult relationships, this shows up as heightened sensitivity to perceived distance, a strong need for reassurance, and intense distress when the relationship is threatened or lost.

> Anxious attachment is not a personality flaw. It is a survival strategy your nervous system developed when it needed one.

## Why breakups hit harder with anxious attachment

For someone with a secure attachment style, a breakup is painful but ultimately manageable - the nervous system can tolerate the loss without going into full alarm. For someone with anxious attachment, the nervous system interprets the loss as a threat to survival. The hypervigilance that was always monitoring the relationship now has nothing to monitor - and goes into overdrive.

The urge to reach out is stronger. The rumination is louder. The grief is more acute. The timeline is longer. None of this means the relationship meant more. It means the attachment system is more activated.

## What this means for recovery

No contact is harder with anxious attachment because the nervous system reads silence as danger. But it is also more necessary - because partial contact keeps the hypervigilant system in a constant state of activation, looking for signals.

> Recovery with anxious attachment takes longer and requires more structure. That is not failure - it is just the actual terrain.
