The cruel thing about ghosting is that it removes the most common path people take toward closure - the final conversation, the explanation, the acknowledged ending. None of that is available to you. And yet the grief still needs somewhere to go.

Closure after ghosting has to be self-generated. That is harder. It is also more durable than the kind that depends on another person.

## Stop waiting for them to come back with an explanation

The most common thing that keeps people stuck after ghosting is a background hope that the ghost will eventually return and explain themselves - and that the explanation will resolve the open loop. It almost never works that way. Even when someone does return, the explanation is usually inadequate. And waiting for it keeps the attachment active for months.

Choosing to stop waiting is not giving up. It is withdrawing your resolution from their hands and taking it back.

> Closure does not require their participation. It requires your decision that you are no longer waiting for it.

## Write the explanation yourself

One of the most effective tools for ghosting recovery is writing your own account of what happened - not the one you wish were true, but the most honest version you can construct from the evidence you have. What did their behaviour tell you? What patterns were there? What does the ghosting itself tell you about who they are and how they handle difficulty?

You will not have all the answers. But you have more than you think. And your account, grounded in what you actually observed, is more useful than an explanation that would probably minimise or deflect anyway.

## Let the unanswered question become answered

The question "why did they ghost me?" has an answer you can reach without them. The answer is: they were not willing or able to show up for a direct conversation. Whatever the specific reasons - avoidance, fear, another person, changed feelings - the mechanism was the same. They prioritised their comfort over your dignity.

> That is enough of an answer to build closure on. You do not need the footnotes.
