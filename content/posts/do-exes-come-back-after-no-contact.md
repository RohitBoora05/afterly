This is one of the most searched questions after a breakup, which tells you something about where people are when they start no contact. The honest answer is: sometimes. And the more useful answer is that this is probably the wrong question to be organising your recovery around.

## What the research actually says

Studies on relationship dissolution and reconciliation suggest that a meaningful percentage of separated couples do reconnect - estimates range widely, but some research puts reunion rates as high as 40 to 50 percent over a multi-year period. Factors that increase the likelihood include relationship length, depth of attachment, absence of a specific betrayal, and whether both people have done genuine work on themselves in the interim.

But these are population-level statistics. They do not tell you anything about your specific situation, your specific ex, or your specific odds.

> Statistics describe patterns across thousands of people. Your relationship is one relationship. The numbers do not apply cleanly.

## Why no contact is not a strategy for getting them back

No contact sometimes results in an ex reaching out. This happens because absence creates space for reflection, because the brain notices what is missing, because the relief of the breakup fades and is replaced by something else. But it is not reliable, it is not within your control, and treating it as a strategy means you are not actually doing no contact - you are doing a waiting game with your healing on hold.

The people who use no contact to try to get their ex back tend to break it when the strategy feels like it is not working. The people who use it to actually heal tend to find that, by the time their ex does or does not come back, they care significantly less about the answer.

## The more useful question

> The question is not whether they come back. It is who you are when they do - or when they do not.

That is what no contact is actually for. Not to create the conditions for a reunion. To create the conditions where you are okay either way.
