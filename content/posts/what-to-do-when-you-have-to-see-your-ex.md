You share a workplace, a class, a friend group, a building. Or there is a wedding, a funeral, a practical matter that cannot be handled by proxy. No contact is the goal - but sometimes seeing them is unavoidable. Here is how to handle it.

## Prepare before, not during

The worst time to decide how you will behave is when you are in the situation. Decide in advance: how you will greet them, how long you will engage, what your exit looks like. Run through it briefly before you go. Not obsessively - just enough to have a plan so you are not improvising from an activated nervous system.

> The conversation you rehearse once is much easier than the one you face cold.

## Aim for neutral, not friendly

The goal is not warmth. It is not coldness either. It is neutral - brief, civil, unrevealing. You do not owe them a performance of how you are doing, in either direction. You do not need to demonstrate that you are fine or that you are suffering. A short, even interaction that gives them nothing to work with is the best outcome.

This is harder than it sounds when the nervous system is activated by their presence. Prepare for that activation - it will happen regardless of how well you are doing - and decide in advance not to let it drive the interaction.

## After the encounter

Expect a spike. Seeing someone you are still attached to will trigger the reward and attachment circuits regardless of how the interaction went. You may feel worse after a neutral encounter than you did before it. That is normal and temporary. Do not make any contact decisions in the hours immediately following.

> Getting through an unavoidable encounter is enough. You do not need to also feel okay about it immediately after.
