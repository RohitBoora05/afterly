No contact gets talked about in two very different ways. In one version, it is a strategy to make your ex miss you and come back. In the other, it is a method for healing. These are not the same thing, and conflating them is why a lot of people end up disappointed.

The honest answer is: no contact works. But what it works for is you - not the outcome you might be hoping for.

## What no contact actually does

When you cut contact completely, two things happen. First, your brain stops receiving the cues that keep the attachment active. The reward circuit built around that person stops getting reinforced. Over time, the baseline begins to shift - the craving quiets, the compulsive checking fades, the acute pain dulls.

Second, you stop generating new emotional inputs tied to them. Every conversation, every seen message, every piece of information about their life is new material for your nervous system to process. No contact removes the feed. The processing can actually finish.

> No contact works because it gives your brain the conditions it needs to detach. Nothing else does.

## What it does not guarantee

No contact does not reliably make your ex come back. Some people do reconnect after a period of no contact. Many do not. Using no contact as a tactic to engineer a specific outcome - rather than as a tool for your own recovery - tends to backfire, because you are measuring success by something you cannot control, and staying emotionally invested in a result rather than in your own healing.

The people who get the most out of no contact are the ones who commit to it for themselves, not as a waiting strategy.

## How long does it need to be?

Research on habit and attachment suggests meaningful neurological change takes around 66 days on average. That is the minimum to aim for - not because of a rule, but because that is roughly how long the brain needs to start building a new baseline. Shorter periods help, but they rarely produce the kind of shift where the urge stops feeling urgent.

> The goal is not to reach a number. The goal is to reach the point where you are no longer running on the old circuitry.
