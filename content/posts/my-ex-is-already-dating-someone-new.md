You found out. Maybe you were looking for it, maybe it found you. Either way, you know now - they are seeing someone else. And however you thought you were doing, this has reset something.

Here is how to move through it.

## What this information does to the brain

Finding out an ex is dating someone new activates the brain's threat response. It introduces a specific kind of loss - not just the relationship, but the hope that the door was still open. The attachment system, which may have been quietly holding onto possibility, receives what it processes as a definitive signal: it is over.

This can feel worse than the original breakup. It is a second loss layered on top of the first. The acute grief response - the physical symptoms, the inability to think about anything else - can restart almost from the beginning.

> What you are feeling right now is proportionate. It is a real loss, not an overreaction.

## What it does not mean

It does not mean the relationship meant nothing to them. People date new people for many reasons - distraction, loneliness, genuine interest, the same withdrawal you are feeling redirected outward. New dating is not a measure of how much someone valued what they had with you.

It also does not mean you need to accelerate your own recovery to keep pace. Your timeline is yours. What they are doing is completely separate from what you need.

## What to do right now

Stop looking. If you found this out through social media or mutual friends, remove the source. Every additional piece of information about their new relationship is new material for your nervous system to process - and none of it helps you heal faster. It only delays it.

> You do not need to know what they are doing. You need to focus on what you are doing. Those are the only updates that matter right now.
