Most advice about moving on implicitly requires you to stop caring about someone - to reframe them as bad, wrong, unworthy of the space they occupy in your thoughts. But what if you do not hate them? What if the relationship ended without a villain, or you genuinely wish them well, and you still need to walk away?

Distance does not require contempt. You can create separation while keeping your own integrity intact.

## Practical separation first

Start with the environment, not the feelings. Unfollow, mute, or block - not as a statement, but as infrastructure. Remove them from the feeds you check daily. Archive the conversation thread. Take their contact out of your recents. These are not acts of hostility. They are friction, and friction helps.

The goal is to stop receiving the involuntary inputs - the posts, the updates, the glimpses of their life - that keep the attachment circuitry active. You are not trying to pretend they do not exist. You are removing them from the parts of your environment where they were never supposed to live anyway.

> Blocking someone is not a declaration of war. It is an act of self-protection that says nothing about them.

## What to do with the warmth

If you still care about them, you do not have to perform indifference. You can hold warmth for someone and still not be in contact with them. You can hope they are well and not need to know whether they are. You can love someone and still choose distance as the most honest response to where things actually are.

## What deletion actually means

It does not mean erasing them from your memory or deciding they never mattered. It means removing them from your active present - from your daily feed, your accessible contacts, the environments where seeing them is a choice you have to keep making. The past stays. You are just not required to keep them in the future.

> You can delete someone from your present without deleting them from your history. They happened. You are just not required to keep them in what comes next.
